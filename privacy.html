Privacy Policy

Office History is built around local storage and user control.

This page describes the data handled by the Office History macOS app and this static website.

Data Stored Locally by the App

Office History stores information on your Mac so it can preserve and display Microsoft Office AutoRecover history. Depending on your use, local data may include:

  • Copies of AutoRecover document artifacts captured as snapshots.
  • Preview text or metadata generated from captured snapshots.
  • Recovery source settings, including the folder you selected.
  • Operational state such as monitoring status, recovery operations, and cleanup records.
  • Local diagnostic logs used to troubleshoot app behavior on your Mac.

What the App Does Not Collect

Office History does not require an account and is not designed to upload your documents, snapshots, filenames, logs, or recovery history to a remote service.

The app does not include third-party analytics, advertising trackers, or cloud sync as part of its core local recovery workflow.

Microsoft Office and AutoRecover

Office History works with the AutoRecover files that Microsoft Word, Excel, and PowerPoint create in their recovery locations. The recommended Word setup is to use a user-managed folder, such as ~/Documents/WordAutorecovery, for both Word and Office History. Excel and PowerPoint recovery locations are detected and confirmed separately.

This Website

This website is a static HTML and CSS site. It does not run a framework, require an account, or include external tracking scripts.

If analytics, crash reporting, contact forms, or other hosted services are added later, this policy should be updated to describe what those services collect and why.

Support

Each app page contains its own support notes. If you report an issue, include only the information needed to diagnose the behavior. Avoid sending private documents, full recovery filenames, or logs unless specifically requested and reviewed by you first.
